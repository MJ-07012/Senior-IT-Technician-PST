Certifications & Their Value

Project Management Professional (PMP)

Importance: The PMP is the gold standard for project management, validating the ability to manage projects, people, and business priorities. It demonstrates a capacity for strategic thinking and execution.

Alignment: This aligns with the need to handle IT projects like technology rollouts or process improvements. My experience in planning and monitoring a technology launch for 8,000 users is a practical application of these skills.

ITIL v4

Importance: ITIL provides a framework for delivering high-quality IT service management (ITSM). It focuses on aligning IT services with business needs and ensuring consistent, repeatable processes.

Alignment: This is directly relevant to setting high standards for service desk activities. My work in implementing process improvements within incident management to meet SLAs reflects the core principles of ITIL.

Certified Scrum Product Owner (CSPO)

Importance: A CSPO understands how to create a product vision, order a product backlog, and ensure the development team delivers maximum value. It emphasizes an agile and user-centric mindset.

Alignment: While this is a technician role, an agile mindset is crucial in a startup environment. It shows I can adapt to change and focus on delivering value to end-users, much like I did when translating support team insights into actionable goals for developers.

Google AI Essentials

Importance: This certification shows a foundational understanding of AI tools and their potential applications. It signals an aptitude for learning and leveraging emerging technologies.

Alignment: For a forward-thinking company like Wiz, staying current on technology is key. This certification demonstrates my commitment to continuous learning and my interest in how new technologies can enhance IT support and operations.
